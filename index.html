01 · Mental model

Closed-loop training, not a one-shot ETL

The pipeline is additive to the existing content platform — every DNA artifact (dna_*.json configs, dna_* tables, /api/v1/dna/* endpoints) is net-new. The loop starts at Kalodata, flows through Gemini vision analysis, lands in Neon as a versioned DNA profile, drives generation configs, gates outputs on compliance, ships to TikTok, and returns performance back to Thompson Sampling bandits that update every attribute arm.

Performance Ingestion

Bandit Update + Drift Detection

learning
  • Reward = 1 if video gpm ≥ P50 baseline for its category, else 0
  • Per attribute value, update Beta: α += reward, β += (1 − reward)
  • Persist dna_bandits(attribute, category, arm_value, α, β)
  • Weekly drift: Jensen-Shannon divergence vs baseline; alert to Slack #dna-alerts if ≥ 0.10
  • Auto-reset baseline after 3 consecutive weeks of same-direction drift — priors rebuild from fresh profile

Ownership split

DNA layer (Neon tables, /api/v1/dna/*) is owned by the ML track. ScriptSage monolith, queue-app, and engines stay under Platform Engineering — DNA reads/writes only through versioned interfaces, never patches in place.

Invariant

No production generation path is rewritten. vertex_standard, pollo_standard, seedance_standard remain the default; DNA configs are routed only by explicit A/B selection or tenant opt-in.

Compliance handoff

Generated videos land in GCS, then are scored against the DNA rubric AND voice-lock composite (≥0.88 production target). Either gate can reject. Rejections feed dna_video_analyses with failure reasons for root-cause analysis.

09 · Reward Loop

How live performance rewires the bandits

Every generated video that hits the ≥1,000 views floor feeds back into a Beta posterior update. This section is the math.

1
Select arms
At job create, each of 8 primary attributes samples from its Beta posterior.
θv ~ Beta(αv, βv)
arm = argmaxv θv
2
Record generation
Selected arm values written to dna_generation_configs alongside profile_date snapshot.
INSERT INTO dna_generation_configs
(job_id, hook_type_selected, …, profile_date)
3
Ingest feedback
Every 6h the TikTok Business API sync writes live GMV/views into dna_performance_feedback.
views ≥ 1000 · signal floor
bandit_updated = FALSE · work queue
4
Map GPM → reward
Binary reward vs. category baseline GPM at the time of generation (not now — avoids drift bias).
r = 1 if gpm ≥ P50category, profile_date
r = 0 otherwise
5
Update posterior
Conjugate update: one success bumps α, one failure bumps β. Cheap, exact, online.
αv′ = αv + r
βv′ = βv + (1 − r)
6
Persist & close loop
Upsert α/β into dna_bandits, mark row processed, next sample uses new posterior.
UPSERT dna_bandits (attribute, category, arm, α, β)
UPDATE dna_performance_feedback SET bandit_updated=TRUE

15 · Rollout & Migration

From 0% canary to steady-state — the 5 promotion ramps

DNA configs never replace standards. They ramp alongside, carrying their own traffic share until they prove out against GMV baselines.

  1. 0%

    Shadow-only

    DNA configs generate variants but nothing ships. Scores are logged for calibration. Duration: 1–2 weeks.

    Promote when: compliance median ≥ 0.75 across 3 categories, voice composite ≥ 0.88 on 100% of chunks.
  2. 10%

    Canary

    10% of new jobs routed to DNA. Standards still receive 90%. Canary side-by-side with a matched control cohort.

    Promote when: 2-week canary shows DNA GMV ≥ standard GMV with p < 0.2, no rubric regression.
  3. 33%

    Split test

    One-third routed to DNA. A/B harness active. Per-category lift tracked separately.

    Promote when: DNA beats standard on GMV-per-product by ≥ +10% in majority of categories at p < 0.1.
  4. 66%

    Default + opt-out

    DNA becomes default; manual override preserves standards. 1-week bake.

    Promote when: zero client escalations, GMV lift sustained, drift alerts clear.
  5. 100%

    Steady-state

    All new jobs go through DNA. Standards kept as emergency rollback target for 90 days.

    Rollback trigger: GMV lift < 0% for 2 consecutive weeks, or P0 additive-guard violation.

16 · Glossary

Every term you'll see in the ledger

Short, operator-grade definitions for the acronyms and domain-specific words used across the pipeline.

DNA
The synthesized profile of what makes top-seller videos work in a category — frequencies, winning arms, and GPM correlations. Not a model. A versioned config object.
GPM
Gross merchandise per mille. Revenue (USD) divided by impressions × 1,000. Core reward signal.
Thompson Sampling
A Bayesian bandit algorithm that samples from each arm's posterior to choose the next draw. Naturally balances explore vs exploit.
Beta posterior
The distribution over success-rate for a single arm. Parameters α (successes) and β (failures). Mean = α/(α+β).
IDS
Indistinguishability Score. How close a generated video is to Kalodata top-sellers across 6 judge ensembles. 0.80+ is ship-ready.
JS divergence
Jensen–Shannon divergence. Symmetric distance between two probability distributions (e.g. last-week profile vs this-week). Drift alert at ≥ 0.10.
Composite rubric
Weighted average across 7 dimensions (hook, duration, caption, music, CTA, demo, B-roll). Ship gate at 0.70.
Voice composite
6-dim voice-lock score: F0 drift, RMS, first-chunk silence, langdetect, embed cosine, crossfade. Gate at 0.88 — strictest gate in the pipeline.
Additive-only
Core invariant: the DNA pipeline may add new tables, configs, endpoints, or columns, but may never modify an existing one. Enforced by CI.
Canary
Low-traffic slice (10%) of live jobs that receive a new policy. Side-by-side with a matched control. First real-world gate after shadow-only.
Shadow run
Generation with scoring but no distribution. Used to calibrate new policies against historic logs before risking real traffic.
Prior blending
When a category has n < 50, its bandit is initialized from a weighted mix of related categories' posteriors. Prevents cold-start thrash.
Drift auto-reset
Bandits reinitialize to profile frequencies after 3 consecutive same-direction JS-divergence weeks. Prevents stale posteriors.
Signal floor
1,000 views minimum before a video's GPM can update any bandit. Keeps low-impression noise out of the learning loop.
Category floor
20 videos minimum before a category has its own profile. Below this, it inherits blended priors.
Champion / Shadow
Champion is the live policy; shadow is the proposed replacement. Shadow runs off-policy until it beats champion statistically.

18 · Self-learning compounder

The generator itself gets better every week, not just the bandits

A bandit can only pick from what the generator produces. For continuous improvement to compound, the generator must absorb the bandit’s winning distribution and re-emit improved variants. Five complementary mechanisms — VideoDPO, RLAIF, ORPO, GAD, and a 3-phase curriculum — turn MomentIQ’s pipeline from template-configured to self-aligning.

5.2

GAD · indistinguishability

quarterly · Nash game

A discriminator learns to tell MomentIQ output from top-creator output. The generator learns to fool it. Unlike static reward models, the discriminator co-evolves — preventing reward hacking. Global optimum = discriminator accuracy approaches 0.5 (chance) = indistinguishable by construction. 

# Adversarial phase
for batch in loop:
  gen = G.generate(batch.prompts)
  # generator reward = how well it fools D
  G_loss = -mean(D(gen))
  G_opt.step(G_loss)
  # update D on fresh G output
  D_loss = BCE(D(real),1) + BCE(D(gen),0)
  D_opt.step(D_loss)

MLE-estimate the hyperprior covariance Σprior from existing brand posteriors. Each new brand inherits the category-specific prior and shrinks toward the hyperprior as data accumulates. # Empirical-Bayes hyperprior
means  = stack(brand_posteriors.values())
Sigma  = cov(means.T)
mu_pri = mean(means, axis=0)

# Hier-TS sample
mu_post, Sig_post = brand_posterior(
  brand_id, mu_pri, Sigma, obs[brand_id]
)
theta = mvn_sample(mu_post, Sig_post)
arm   = argmax(theta)

6.2

UCB portfolio allocator · ROAS floor

Global constraint: sum(spend) ≤ B_total and portfolio_ROAS ≥ 2.0. UCB score per brand = μ̂roas + √(2 log t / n). Sorted-greedy allocation to brands with highest UCB until budget exhausted. Brands below the ROAS floor are automatically budget-starved. 

for t in weeks:
  ucb = {b: mu[b] + sqrt(2*log(t)/n[b])
         for b in brands}
  # sorted greedy
  for b in sorted(ucb, key=ucb.get, reverse=True):
    alloc[b] = min(rem, rem * ucb[b]/sum(ucb.values()))
    rem -= alloc[b]
  # PID pacing trim

16 · Failure modes & safety rails

Seven failure classes, twelve circuit breakers, three kill switches

An autonomous pipeline that can spend a brand's budget without human approval must assume every component will fail in an adversarial direction. Goodhart's law, reward hacking, mode collapse, data gaming, drift, compliance drift, cascade failures — we catalog 30 failure modes across 7 classes, define formulas for each detection signal, and wire autonomous recoveries. Humans only at severity S1 or repeated S2.

F1

Reward hacking

Goodhart's law: the judge becomes the objective.

  • Judge/GMV divergence: |Rjudge − RGMV| > 2σ over 7-day window
  • Sycophantic judge: corr(judge_score, brand_prior) > 0.8
  • Spec gaming: reward ↑ while GMV-attribution ↓
  • Adversarial prompt exploits: monthly red-team tournament
  • Remedy: ORPO χ² regularization + RLAIF cross-check + shadow reward model
F2

Distribution shift

Seasonal, category-migration, concept drift.

  • PSI > 0.25 warn, > 0.5 alert
  • KS test on reward dist: p < 0.01 over 24h
  • MMD on embeddings: > threshold per-category
  • Conformal p-values trending low
  • Remedy: auto-retrain hyperpriors + canary freeze + escalate if persistent
F3

Model collapse

Generator or discriminator degenerates.

  • Effective arm count: 1/Σpi² < 3 for 72h
  • Discriminator trivial: AUC → 1.0 (generator too weak)
  • DPO overfit: train↔eval KL > 0.5
  • Catastrophic forgetting: baseline regression on held-out set
  • Remedy: DPP diversity penalty + ref-policy rollback + 10% replay buffer
F4

Data poisoning / gaming

Adversarial creators attack Kalodata feed.

  • GMV velocity spike 10× baseline, no paid push
  • Unverified seller (age <180d, return-rate ≥5%)
  • Kalodata↔Fastmoss disagreement on same video
  • Click-farm fingerprint in watch-time distribution
  • Remedy: multi-source cross-check + velocity filter + hard-negative mining
F5

Cold-start catastrophe

New brands/categories misrouted.

  • Flat Beta(1,1) prior detected on any arm
  • Brand has <5 similar neighbors in prior index
  • L3 category new & unsupervised
  • Remedy: fallback chain brand→nearest-neighbor→L3→L2→L1→platform; never flat
F6

Infra / pipeline

Backpressure, staleness, DDoS, cascades.

  • Kafka consumer lag > 10 min
  • OPE estimate staleness > 6h
  • Neon replica lag > 90s
  • Rate-limit cascade signal across >2 services
  • Remedy: circuit breakers + KEDA scale-out + graceful degradation to baseline
F7

Governance / compliance

FTC, TikTok policy, brand safety, PII.

  • FTC disclosure missing in any promoted variant
  • TikTok policy update → constitution diff > 0
  • Brand-safety classifier flag
  • PII/copyright fingerprint in prompt or output
  • Remedy: constitutional AI gate + human-in-loop on any red-flag + instant rollback
